Generations Of MobilePhones

Mobile networks are effectively a web of what's known as base stations.
These base stations each cover a specific geographical land area-called a cell-and are equipped with at least one fixed-location transceiver antenna that enables the cell to send and receive transmissions between devices using radio waves.
Users can make and receive phone calls and some cellphones also offer text messaging.
A smartphone has more advanced features, including web browsing, software applications and a mobile OS.
Smartphones also offer capablities such as biometrics support, video chatting and virtual assistants.
